Cardiology Quick Reference — Cardiac Conduction & Hemodynamics

The sinoatrial (SA) node is located in the upper wall of the right atrium near the
superior vena cava. It is the heart's primary pacemaker, firing at 60-100 bpm.
The atrioventricular (AV) node sits in the interatrial septum near the tricuspid
valve and delays the impulse ~0.1 s so the atria can finish contracting before
ventricular systole. The bundle of His conducts the impulse into the ventricles,
splitting into right and left bundle branches; Purkinje fibers distribute the
signal through the ventricular myocardium, producing a synchronized contraction.

Cardiac output (CO) = heart rate x stroke volume. Normal resting CO is ~5 L/min.
Stroke volume depends on preload (Frank-Starling: increased end-diastolic volume
stretches myocytes and raises contractile force), afterload (aortic pressure the
ventricle must overcome), and contractility. Ejection fraction = SV / EDV; normal
is 55-70%. An EF below 40% indicates systolic heart failure.

On the ECG: the P wave = atrial depolarization, the QRS complex = ventricular
depolarization, the T wave = ventricular repolarization. The PR interval
(normally 0.12-0.20 s) reflects AV nodal delay. A widened QRS (>0.12 s) suggests
bundle branch block or a ventricular origin rhythm.
